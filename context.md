*Last updated: February 2026*

## What Lectere Is

Lectere is an AI-powered software guidance tool that appears **inside any software application** and shows users exactly what to do, step by step, directly on their screen. It eliminates the need for tutorials, help docs, IT support tickets, or tab-switching — by detecting the user's context and rendering real-time visual overlays that guide them through any task.

**Tagline:** "Guidance that adapts to you."
**Mission:** Making technology accessible to all.
**Website:** lectere.com

## The Problem: The Forced Adoption Crisis

Society is rapidly mandating digital participation — government services, healthcare, business compliance, and work — but has left people without adequate support to keep up. This is not a access problem; it's a **usability and learning problem** for people who already have technology but can't effectively use it.

### Why Existing Solutions Fail
- **Help documentation** — outdated the moment UI changes
- **YouTube tutorials** — generic, never matches the user's actual screen
- **IT support / training courses** — slow, expensive, or nonexistent for individuals and small businesses
- **ChatGPT / Google** — abstract answers that don't map to what's on screen

### Key Statistics
- **74% of users abandon complex software** (Gartner, 2022)
- **76% of users give up** when trying to learn new software on their own (Gartner, 2022)
- **$8.5 trillion** projected annual cost of digital skill gaps to the global economy by 2030 (World Economic Forum, 2023)
- **40% productivity loss** from context-switching between work and tutorials (Harvard Business Review, 2024)
- **71% of adults 50+** are interested in tech support tailored to older users, yet most software is not designed with them in mind (AARP, 2024)
- **55% of frontline workers** say they've had to learn new tech on the fly (Cornerstone, 2025)
- Small businesses spend **$1,091 per employee on training** in 2025 (LearnExperts / Training Mag, 2025)
- Organizations fail to meet expectations with technology implementation **50% of the time** (Cornerstone)
- Companies worldwide waste an average of **$1.4 trillion** annually on digital transformation projects that fail to deliver impact

## How Lectere Works

**Step 1 — Contextual Detection**
Lectere understands what software the user is in and where they are within it relative to their query.

**Step 2 — On-Screen Guidance**
A visual cursor overlay shows the user exactly where to click, with plain-language explanations — no tab switching, no searching.

**Step 3 — Automatic Adaptation**
Unlike static docs or tutorials, Lectere continuously adapts to UI changes, keeping guidance current automatically.

### Two Modes
- **Guidance Mode** — User performs the actions themselves; Lectere provides real-time visual overlays showing exactly what to do. This is the primary mode shown in demos.
- **Execution Mode** — Lectere acts on the user's behalf (training wheels). Builds model understanding of workflows to power guidance mode more accurately over time.

## Technical Architecture

- **Core:** Rust for business logic, Swift 6 + SwiftUI for native macOS interface
- **Screen capture:** ScreenCaptureKit (targeting 60 FPS)
- **AI model:** UI-TARS-2 via HuggingFace API (potential migration to local inference on Mac Studio M3 Ultra post-launch)
- **Backend:** Supabase (PostgreSQL) for auth, user management, subscription tracking
- **Distribution:** Direct from lectere.com (requires Screen Recording + Accessibility permissions — not App Store)
- **Step detection:** AXObserver API (event-driven, no polling)

## Key Framing Principles

1. **Forced adoption crisis, not digital divide** — Lectere targets people who *have* technology but struggle with *complex software*, not those who lack access entirely.
2. **Learning tool, not a crutch** — Execution mode is training wheels. The goal is for users to need Lectere less over time as they build proficiency.
3. **Always current** — Unlike static docs or tutorials, Lectere never breaks when software updates.
4. **Works across all software** — Not limited to one app or one platform, unlike every competitor.
